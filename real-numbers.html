Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Complete Notes & Exercise-wise Solutions

📘 Introduction to Real Numbers

Real numbers include both rational and irrational numbers. In this chapter, we study Euclid's Division Lemma, HCF, LCM, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, and properties of irrational numbers.

📌 Important Formulas

  • a = bq + r (Euclid's Division Lemma)
  • HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers
  • Every composite number has unique prime factorization
  • Rational numbers have terminating or recurring decimals

Exercise 1.1 Solutions

This exercise is based on Euclid's Division Algorithm.

To find HCF, we repeatedly apply:

a = bq + r until remainder becomes zero.

Example Type: Find HCF of two numbers using division method.

Exercise 1.2 Solutions

Based on Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic.

Every composite number can be written as a product of prime numbers.

Use prime factorization method to find HCF and LCM.

Exercise 1.3 Solutions

This exercise proves irrationality of numbers like √2, √3, √5.

Method: Assume number is rational → express as p/q → square both sides → reach contradiction.

Exercise 1.4 Solutions

Here we check whether a rational number has terminating decimal expansion.

If denominator has only 2 and/or 5 as prime factors → decimal terminates.

Exercise 1.5 Summary

  • Euclid’s Lemma is used to find HCF.
  • Prime factorization helps in finding LCM.
  • Irrational numbers cannot be written as p/q.
  • Decimal expansion rule depends on denominator.
